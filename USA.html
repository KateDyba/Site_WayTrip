USA

The United States of America is an alluring and multifaceted country in North America. It includes 50 states. They snug between the Appalachian Mountain range in the east and the snow-white cold-desert of Alaska in the north-west. The USA is the world’s third-largest country according to its territory. The territory of 9,5 million km² comprises plenty of historical attractions, museums and art galleries, amusement parks, beach and skiing resorts, unique natural reserves. But the special charm of the USA is hidden in the deep countryside, in small villages, and in agricultural areas.

    Arizona

    Arizona is a state of great canyons located in the southwestern part of the United States. Most of its territory is occupied by massive rocky mountains with dim white foothills, fantastic reddish deserts, majestic canyons. It is here that the famous Grand Canyon is located, which tourists from all over the world come to see. Among the desert landscapes of Arizona, there are bright spots of incredibly beautiful green valleys. Here you can see the life of real Indian tribes, admire buildings of Spanish and Mexican architecture, visit unique museums, and appreciate traditional American cuisine.

    Alaska

    Alaska is a bright, harsh, and overwhelming kingdom of ice, endless forests, and enormous lakes that reflect the mountains' snowy peaks. It is worth coming here at least to see with your own eyes what you read about in the novels of Jack London — the magical beauty of this region. Breathtaking walks through the reserves, tiring ascents to glaciers, dizzying river rafting — Alaska nature will give you all this.

    Brooklyn Bridge

    The Brooklyn Bridge is a hybrid cable-stayed/suspension bridge in New York City, spanning the East River between the boroughs of Manhattan and Brooklyn. Bridge was the first fixed crossing of the East River. It was also the longest suspension bridge in the world at the time of its opening, with a main span of 1,595.5 feet (486.3 m) and a deck 127 ft (38.7 m) above mean high water. The Brooklyn Bridge is the southernmost of the four toll-free vehicular bridges connecting Manhattan Island and Long Island, with the Manhattan Bridge, the Williamsburg Bridge, and the Queensboro Bridge to the north. Only passenger vehicles and pedestrian and bicycle traffic are permitted. A major tourist attraction since its opening, the Brooklyn Bridge has become an icon of New York City. Over the years, the bridge has been used as the location of various stunts and performances, as well as several crimes and attacks. The Brooklyn Bridge has been designated a National Historic Landmark, a New York City landmark, and a National Historic Civil Engineering Landmark. 

    California

    California is a sunny state on the west coast of the United States. It is surrounded by an aura of creativity, wealthy carefree life, and eternal summer.

    There is no need to talk much about California because it has been shown millions of times in movies, described in books, and mentioned in songs. For once in your life, you need to be here to understand why so many people are willing to leave their usual lives for a ghostly chance to become rich and famous.
