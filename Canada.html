Canada

Canada, the second largest country in the world in area, occupying roughly the northern two-fifths of the continent of North America. Despite Canada’s great size, it is one of the world’s most sparsely populated countries. Canada's capital is Ottawa and its three largest metropolitan areas are Toronto, Montreal, and Vancouver.

    Yukon

    Yukon became a point of worldwide attention in the late XIX century, as soon as the first gold pieces were found on the Klondike River. But when those glorious times were over, it turned out that this Canadian territory's main treasure is not precious metal at all. Yukon residents truly value their dramatic and splendid scenery, the history full of vivid events, and the first settlers' unique culture. Tourists strive here to see the midnight sun and northern lights with their own eyes, plunge into the atmosphere of the gold rush, and fall in love with this region's wildlife forever.

    Alberta

    Alberta is Canada's fourth-largest province after Quebec, Ontario, and British Columbia. Since the XVII century, it has attracted researchers and colonizers with its natural treasures and diversity of animals. Even though there are two large cities here, Alberta is primarily a taiga and endless prairies, where you can go on an adventure away from civilization and the everlasting hustle and bustle. But this Canadian province is not as simple as it seems at first sight. It can surprise with an unusual shopping center, traditional rodeos, crazy festivals, and unique museums.

    Banff National Park

    Banff National Park is one of Canada's top natural attractions. Founded in 1885, it is the first and also the largest reserve in the country - its area is 6641 square meters. km. Since 1984 it has been on the UNESCO World Heritage List. Banff Park is located in the province of Alberta, west of the town of Calgary, in the Rocky Mountains of Canada. It was created to protect the diverse and unique landscape, as well as to develop the health and tourism infrastructure of the region. Every year, Banff National Park is visited by 4 million tourists from all over the world. Majestic mountains, dense coniferous forests, rich flora and fauna, bewitching glaciers, alpine landscapes, pristine lakes - this is a brief description of the beauties that can be observed here. 

    Toronto

    The Toronto Islands - also called the Island, or Toronto Island Park is located in Lake Ontario, a 13-minute ferry ride from downtown Toronto. Boats to the Island leave from the Jack Layton Ferry Terminal at the foot of Bay St.

    Islands

    The Island is actually a group of 15 islands inter-connected by pathways and bridges. You can walk from one end of the Island to the other. The island is approximately 5kms long from Ward's Island to Hanlan's Point.
