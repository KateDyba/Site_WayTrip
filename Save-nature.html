It's no secret that we are now living in an era of great ecological problems. It's becoming increasingly clear that there are fewer and fewer plants and animals left on our planet. And in most cases we are to blame. 
The problem of ecology is one of the global problems of the mankind on the Earth. Pollution of water reservoirs by sewage, air pollution by exhaust gases from cars and factories, pollution of forests, parks and squares by household garbage.

Over the past 500 years, 844 species of animals and plants have become extinct. This is mainly due to the fault of man. Humans purposefully exterminate these species by shooting, destroying them with poison baits in order to get valuable furs, skins, skins, meat or just for fun. Man destroys nature's plants by plucking them out by the handfuls and then throwing them away. A large number of forests are destroyed because of fires that are left unextinguished.

Cleaning up Ocean Pollution

It is critical that we, as residents on this planet, join together to fight ocean trash. There are multiple agencies, nonprofits, and corporations who are joining the fight, and we can support them.
The International Coastal Cleanup organization started more than 30 years ago, when communities came together to collect and document the trash along their Texas coastlines.

The organization connected with the Texas General Land Office, local businessmen and women, and other ocean-lovers, and planned what would be Ocean Conservancy’s first Cleanup. Volunteers didn’t just pick up trash; they recorded each item collected on a data card in order to help find ways to eradicate ocean trash moving forward.
